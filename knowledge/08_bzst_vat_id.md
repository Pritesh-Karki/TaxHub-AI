# BZSt Guidance — VAT Identification Number (USt-IdNr.)

**Source:** Bundeszentralamt für Steuern (BZSt), guidance on applying for a VAT
identification number
**URL:** https://www.bzst.de/DE/Unternehmen/Identifikationsnummern/Umsatzsteuer_Identifikationsnummer/umsatzsteuer_identifikationsnummer_node.html

## Guidance (excerpt, translated)

The VAT identification number (Umsatzsteuer-Identifikationsnummer, USt-IdNr.) is issued by
the BZSt on written or electronic application and is distinct from the domestic tax number
(Steuernummer) issued by the local Finanzamt. It is required for intra-EU transactions,
including Zusammenfassende Meldungen (recapitulative statements) and the reverse-charge
mechanism on cross-border B2B supplies within the EU.

A Kleinunternehmer under Section 19 UStG may still apply for and hold a USt-IdNr., since
holding one does not itself trigger standard VAT taxation; it is commonly needed for
intra-Community acquisitions or for using online marketplaces that require it for
compliance purposes, independent of the entrepreneur's domestic VAT treatment.

The BZSt validates USt-IdNr. numbers of EU trading partners via the qualified confirmation
procedure (qualifizierte Bestätigungsanfrage), which German entrepreneurs should use before
zero-rating an intra-Community supply to document good faith.

## Practical note

Advisors should not assume a client's use of a USt-IdNr. implies they have waived
Kleinunternehmer status; the two are independent and should be checked separately.
