# AO §147 — Retention of Books and Records (Aufbewahrungspflichten)

**Source:** Abgabenordnung (AO), Section 147
**URL:** https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/ao_1977/__147.html

## Text (excerpt, translated)

Books, inventories, annual financial statements, management reports, and the accounting
records underlying them (Buchungsbelege, i.e. invoices and receipts) must be retained for
ten years. Received and sent commercial and business letters, and other documents relevant
for taxation, must be retained for six years. Retention periods for accounting records were
shortened to eight years for records created from 1 January 2025 onward under recent
simplification legislation, with transitional rules for records predating that change.

Records may be retained in electronic form (e.g. as scanned images) provided they remain
fully legible, unalterable, and can be made available without delay, machine-readable, and
evaluable by the tax authority during an audit (Section 147(2), (6)); the original paper
document may generally then be discarded, subject to exceptions for documents legally
required in original form.

## Practical note

The shortened eight-year retention period for accounting records (Buchungsbelege) applies
prospectively; advisors should confirm which retention period governs records created before
versus after the 1 January 2025 cutoff when advising on document destruction schedules.
