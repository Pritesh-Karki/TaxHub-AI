# EStG §4 Abs. 3 — Cash-Basis Accounting (Einnahmenüberschussrechnung)

**Source:** Einkommensteuergesetz (EStG), Section 4(3)
**URL:** https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/estg/__4.html

## Text (excerpt, translated)

Taxpayers not obligated to keep books and prepare regular financial statements, and who do
not do so voluntarily, may determine profit as the surplus of operating income over
operating expenses (Einnahmenüberschussrechnung, EÜR). Income and expenses are generally
recognized under the cash-received/cash-paid principle (Zufluss-/Abflussprinzip) of Section
11.

Depreciable fixed assets (abnutzbares Anlagevermögen) are an exception: acquisition or
production costs are not deducted in full at payment but must be capitalized and depreciated
over the asset's useful life (Absetzung für Abnutzung), consistent with the treatment under
balance-sheet accounting.

Businesses exceed the EÜR eligibility threshold and must switch to double-entry bookkeeping
where annual turnover exceeds EUR 800,000 or annual profit exceeds EUR 80,000 (thresholds as
amended by the Wachstumschancengesetz, effective for fiscal years beginning after 31
December 2023).

## Practical note

Recurring payments received or paid shortly before or after year-end (within 10 days, under
the "kurze Zeit" rule referencing Section 11(1) sentence 2 and (2) sentence 2) are attributed
to the year they economically belong to, not strictly the year of cash movement.
