# UStG §14 Abs. 4 — Mandatory Invoice Contents

**Source:** Umsatzsteuergesetz (UStG), Section 14(4)
**URL:** https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/ustg_1980/__14.html

## Text (excerpt, translated)

An invoice must contain: the full name and address of the supplying entrepreneur and of the
recipient; the tax number or VAT identification number of the supplying entrepreneur; the
date of issue; a sequential invoice number that is unique and assigned once
(fortlaufende Nummer); the quantity and commercial description of the goods or the scope and
nature of the service; the date of supply or service, or the date payment was received if
that date is fixed and differs from the invoice date; the consideration broken down by tax
rate and any exemption, plus any agreed reduction not already reflected in the consideration;
the applicable tax rate and the resulting tax amount, or a reference to an applicable tax
exemption.

From 1 January 2025, domestic B2B transactions between resident entrepreneurs generally
require a structured electronic invoice (E-Rechnung) meeting the EN 16931 standard, subject
to transitional rules through 2027 permitting other formats by mutual agreement.

## Practical note

For invoices under EUR 250 (Kleinbetragsrechnung), Section 33 UStDV relaxes these
requirements: a sequential invoice number and the recipient's name/address are not required,
though the tax rate and gross amount must still be stated.
