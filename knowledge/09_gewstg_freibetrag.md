# GewStG §11 Abs. 1 — Trade Tax Allowance (Freibetrag) for Individuals and Partnerships

**Source:** Gewerbesteuergesetz (GewStG), Section 11(1)
**URL:** https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/gewstg/__11.html

## Text (excerpt, translated)

For sole proprietors (Einzelunternehmer) and partnerships (Personengesellschaften), trade tax
is assessed on the trade income (Gewerbeertrag) reduced by an allowance of EUR 24,500 before
the applicable rate (Steuermesszahl) is applied. The allowance does not apply to
corporations (Kapitalgesellschaften), which are subject to trade tax from the first euro of
trade income.

Trade income is derived from income determined under the Income Tax Act or Corporate Income
Tax Act, adjusted by specific additions (Hinzurechnungen, e.g. a portion of interest expense
and rent/lease payments under Section 8) and reductions (Kürzungen, e.g. for real property
under Section 9) particular to trade tax.

## Practical note

Freelancers (Freiberufler under Section 18 EStG, e.g. many tax advisors, doctors, and
consultants providing personal professional services) are generally not subject to trade tax
at all, as their activity does not constitute a Gewerbebetrieb; this allowance and the tax
itself are relevant only to commercial trades and businesses.
