# BMF Letter — Kleinunternehmerregelung and Cross-Border EU Small Business Scheme

**Source:** Bundesministerium der Finanzen (BMF), guidance letter on the 2025 reform of
Section 19 UStG implementing the EU small business scheme (Directive (EU) 2020/285)
**URL:** https://www.bundesfinanzministerium.de/Content/DE/Downloads/BMF_Schreiben/Steuerarten/Umsatzsteuer/2024-12-18-anwendungsfragen-kleinunternehmerregelung.html

## Guidance (excerpt, translated)

From 1 January 2025, the small business regulation is extended: entrepreneurs resident in
another EU member state may also apply the German Kleinunternehmerregelung for domestic
German turnover, provided their EU-wide annual turnover does not exceed EUR 100,000 and they
hold a valid small-business identification number issued under the special notification
procedure in their state of establishment.

Conversely, a German-resident small business may use the corresponding scheme to supply
other EU member states VAT-free, subject to that state's own national threshold and to
registration via the Bundeszentralamt für Steuern (BZSt) special notification procedure, with
quarterly turnover reporting.

The BMF clarifies that the EUR 100,000 EU-wide ceiling is a hard cap: exceeding it during the
year ends eligibility for the cross-border scheme immediately, from the point the threshold
is crossed, rather than at the following year-end as applies to the purely domestic
provision.

## Practical note

Advisors should distinguish the domestic threshold check (EUR 25,000 / EUR 100,000, prior
year / current year) from the separate EU-wide cross-border ceiling (EUR 100,000, immediate
cut-off), as the two are checked differently and apply to different fact patterns.
