# EStG §4 Abs. 5 Satz 1 Nr. 6b, 6c — Home Office Deduction

**Source:** Einkommensteuergesetz (EStG), Section 4(5) sentence 1 no. 6b and 6c
**URL:** https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/estg/__4.html

## Text (excerpt, translated)

Expenses for a home office and the costs of its furnishings are generally not deductible.
An exception applies where no other workspace is available for the professional or business
activity: in that case, expenses are deductible up to EUR 1,260 per year (the "Homeoffice-
Pauschale" ceiling as amended for assessment periods from 2023 onward), or without ceiling
if the home office forms the center of the taxpayer's entire professional and business
activity.

Independently of whether a dedicated home office qualifies, taxpayers may claim a daily
flat-rate home-office allowance ("Tagespauschale") of EUR 6 per calendar day worked
predominantly from home, up to a maximum of EUR 1,260 per year (210 days), for assessment
periods from 2023 onward. This flat rate is available even where no separate home office
room exists, provided no other permanent workplace was available on that day, or where the
taxpayer works from home despite having another workplace available, subject to certain
conditions.

## Practical note

The daily flat rate (Tagespauschale) and the dedicated-room deduction cannot both be claimed
for the same days. Taxpayers should track which days were worked from home to substantiate
the Tagespauschale claim if not maintaining a qualifying dedicated room.
