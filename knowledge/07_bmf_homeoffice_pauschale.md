# BMF Letter — Application of the Homeoffice-Pauschale and Tagespauschale

**Source:** Bundesministerium der Finanzen (BMF), guidance letter on the home-office flat
rate following the Jahressteuergesetz 2022 reform
**URL:** https://www.bundesfinanzministerium.de/Content/DE/Downloads/BMF_Schreiben/Steuerarten/Einkommensteuer/2023-01-15-steuerliche-anerkennung-erwerbsbedingte-aufwendungen-homeoffice.html

## Guidance (excerpt, translated)

The daily flat rate of EUR 6 (Tagespauschale) is available per calendar day on which the
taxpayer worked predominantly (mehr als die Hälfte der Tagesarbeitszeit) in their home, and
did not on that same day visit a permanent first place of work (erste Tätigkeitsstätte).
Where a taxpayer has no other workplace available at all for the relevant activity, the daily
rate is available even on days including some outside travel, as long as no permanent first
workplace exists.

The flat rate is granted per person, not per household: two taxpayers sharing a home office
may each claim it independently for their own qualifying days, provided each meets the
conditions in their own right.

The BMF confirms that the Tagespauschale and the actual-cost dedicated-room deduction under
Section 4(5) sentence 1 no. 6c are mutually exclusive for the same days, but a taxpayer may
use the dedicated-room deduction for days meeting that room's stricter "center of activity"
test and the Tagespauschale for other qualifying days within the same year.

## Practical note

Advisors should ask clients whether they share a home office with a spouse or partner who
also works from home, since each person's entitlement is calculated separately.
